Sulphur

Symbol: S
Atomic Number: 16
Atomic Mass: 32.065 u
Colour: Yellow
Odour: Rotten egg smell, especially when combined with hydrogen to form hydrogen sulfide.
State (STP): Solid
Category: Non - Metal
Melting Point: 115.21 °C
Boiling Point: 444.6 °C
Density: 2.07 g/cm³.
Fun Fact: Sulphur is primarily employed as a reactant in manufacturing sulfuric acid (H₂SO₄). Sulphuric acid is the most prevalent mass chemical in industrialised nations, and it is utilised in massive quantities in lead-acid batteries for vehicles.
